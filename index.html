Our Mission: An Open-Source Lunar City

Our goal is ambitious yet crucial: to collaboratively design and develop a fully open-source lunar base, one essential subsystem at a time. We believe that an open, shared approach is the fastest and most effective way to enable a sustainable human presence on the Moon.

By making the designs, simulation tools, and processes open, we empower a global community of engineers, scientists, students, and enthusiasts to contribute, innovate, and build upon this foundational work for the future of humanity in space.

LunCoSim: The Simulation & Design Engine

LunCoSim (Lunar Colony Simulator) is our core product and the virtual proving ground for the Open Lunar Base. Built with the powerful Godot 4 engine, it's specifically designed for engineering complex space systems, especially lunar settlements.

This isn't just a simulator; it's the integrated development environment where we design, test, visualize, and integrate each component of the lunar base. Key features include:

  • Realistic 3D Lunar Environment & Physics
  • Collaborative, Multi-user Design Sessions
  • Subsystem Modeling & Simulation (Power, Life Support, etc.)
  • Requirements Management & Integration
  • Digital Twin Capabilities for the Lunar Base
  • Support for Open Standards
  • Web3 Integration with NFT-based access and sharing
  • Login via wallet and tokenized gating

We're revolutionizing Space Engineering by creating an immersive, Web3-integrated metaverse for designing lunar settlements and bridging the gap between professional tools and modern gaming.

Currently, our efforts are centered on implementing Supply Chain modeling via Graph View and Modelica integration, an object-oriented language to model cyber-physical systems. These powerful tools enable us to create interactive immersive simulation experiences in our 3D environment.
